University of Maryland Eruv

The University of Maryland Eruv surrounds the entire campus as well as some housing off campus, encompassing Hillel, Chabad, and much of College Park. The Eruv was founded in the early 2000s and has been going strong ever since.


Batya Teitz, Gavriel Epstein and Eitan Griboff boldly look out into the unknown.

The Eruv is currently maintained by Gavriel Epstein, '20. He and his brave crew daringly face the streets and telephone poles of College Park in order to make sure that the community is able to carry over the coming Shabbat.

The UMD Eruv is checked every Friday morning and any needed maintenance is taken care of. After the check is complete, the UMD Eruv Facebook page posts that the Eruv is up.

The Eruv Committee is always looking for volunteers to check drivers to drive. If you are interested in helping out the community, please contact Gavriel Epstein on Facebook.

Don't know how to check? Don't know how to drive? We'd love to teach you! (At least, we can teach you the checking part; for driving you'll want someone else's help.) The Eruv Committee checks each week, and repairs are often needed. Joining the Eruv committee is a great way to give back to the community in a way that is extremely underappreciated considering the community's dependence on the Eruv.
